5. PERMITTED USES
================================================================================

Employees of {org_name} are authorized to use {tool_name} for:

  a) {category} tasks directly related to their approved job functions;
  b) Drafting, reviewing, and summarizing non-confidential documents;
  c) Generating ideas or suggestions, provided a human validates all outputs
     before any decision-making or external communication;
  d) Internal technical tasks that do not involve personally identifiable
     information of customers, employees, or third parties;
  e) Training and experimentation using fictional or anonymized data.

================================================================================
6. PROHIBITED USES
================================================================================

Employees are strictly prohibited from using {tool_name} to:

  a) Process sensitive personal information (health data, financial records,
     disciplinary or criminal records) without protective controls and a prior
     Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA);
  b) Make final decisions about individuals (hiring, credit, service access,
     performance evaluations) without mandatory human review and a mechanism
     for the individual to challenge the decision;
  c) Share trade secrets, protected intellectual property, confidential
     contracts, or customer data with the system;
  d) Circumvent internal control, audit, or compliance processes;
  e) Generate, distribute, or amplify misleading, discriminatory, or harmful
     content;
  f) Any use contrary to Law 25 (Quebec), PIPEDA, GDPR where applicable, or
     any other data protection legislation.

================================================================================
7. TRANSPARENCY OBLIGATIONS — LAW 25 ART. 12.1 (QUEBEC)
================================================================================

Pursuant to Article 12.1 of Quebec's Law 25, if {tool_name} is used to make
decisions based exclusively on automated processing of personal information that
produces legal or significant effects on an individual, {org_name} must:

  • Inform the individual that their personal information is used for automated
    decision-making;
  • Communicate which personal information was used in the decision;
  • Offer the individual the right to have the decision reviewed by a human;
  • Allow the individual to present their observations.

Refer to the Automated Decision Disclosure Notice generated separately for the
required disclosure text.
